Costume Design

Created by Naomi Mau (Including Drawings)

Main Characters

Ginger

  • main color is green (like fresh grass)
    • green trousers
    • green bandana/scarf
    • green hair ribbon (instead of a cap)
  • fringe feathers instead of faux feathers (we do not want to disrespect cultures who use feathers in their traditional clothing)

Rocky

  • Though all the chickens are American in this rendition of Chicken Run, Rocky still is exudes the red, white and blue colors
  • fringe feathers; however, because he is a rooster, his outfit will be a little bigger and bolder

Mrs. Tweedy

  • 1930-1950s fashion, but make it dark, dreary and drab; austere hairstyle

Mr. Tweedy

  • typical 1930s - 1950s farmer look
  • needs to match Mrs. Tweedy slightly, so he wears darker colors as well
    • Light shirt, dark overalls, straw hat, dark flannel shirt

Supporting Characters

General Chicken Costume

  • balloon trousers to emphasize the tiny “chicken legs”
  • fringe feathers
  • pastel bandana
  • vest that is longer in the back; covered in fringe in the back
  • yellow undershirt and shoes
  • colors may vary between chicken, but no chicken should upstage Ginger and Rocky in color.

Babs

  • general chicken costume
  • yarn and knitting needles as props; knitted bandana

Bunty

  • general chicken costume
  • knitted cap; beaded necklace

Mac

  • general chicken costume
  • pen and paper props
  • braided hair
  • optional glasses (not pictured)

Fowler

  • professional army-style costume (in a similar style to the general chicken costume)
  • air force badges and medals

Nick and Fletcher

  • fun, “newsie” style costumes including a vest and cap each
  • humans dressed as rats, but not hyper realistic

Edwina

  • general chicken costume
  • Note: the actress who plays Edwina becomes a regular chicken after Edwina’s death. Edwina will be characterized with a knitted shawl before she dies. The shawl is removed afterwards as the actress becomes a regular chicken.

Circus Man

  • ringmaster costume

Extra Chickens

  • general chicken costume in various, though more muted, colors
  • muted, pastel/floral pattern bandanas

Dogs

  • guard costumes
  • humans dressed as dogs: ear flaps and tail
