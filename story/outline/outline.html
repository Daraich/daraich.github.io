Outline

A reimagining of the animated film Chicken Run (2000), but with an American twist, and music!

Setting

A factory farm during peak industrial revolution America.

Notes

Mrs. Tweedy is now a Southern Belle still in pursuit of cold hard cash/profits with her highly exploitative capitalist business practices; still wants to turn the chickens into pies so she can get rich.

Scene 1: Freedom, Where Art Thou?

By Raymond Sanchez

  • Introductory music: Mission Impossible. Elaborate escape plans (that never work), all chickens needed. This is a chaotic number: all animals (chickens minus Rocky, rats, dogs) need to appear
  • Mr. Tweedy catches Ginger trying to escape the coop; Mrs. Tweedy appears
  • Ginger is thrown in solitary confinement (by Mr. Tweedy)
  • The following morning Ginger watches from her “cell” as the other chickens haplessly try to escape the coop
  • Ginger is released (from solitary confinement)
  • She and the chickens are lined up by Fowler for a headcount that turns into an egg count
  • Mrs. Tweedy appears, taking note of egg production counts
  • Edwina, who has not produced any eggs is taken
  • Edwina is killed as Ginger watches from a rooftop
  • Ginger expresses the need to escape as she cries (still on rooftop) and watches as birds fly overhead
  • DD: Ginger: [sighing] “We've gotta get out of here.”
  • Gingers “I WANT” song (along the lines of “Somewhere Over the Rainbow”)
  • Ginger and another chicken exchange plans to organize that night

Scene 2: Secret Meeting

By Raymond Sanchez

  • The chickens gather in hut # for a meeting
  • Ginger meets with the rats (before arriving at the meeting) to acquire materials/resources needed to build a contraption for escaping
  • They ultimately do not help her because they want eggs in exchange for the materials (eggs are too valuable)
  • Scene cuts to interior of the Tweedy house/office… Mrs. Tweedy sees an ad for a Pie Machine… Mr. Tweedy believes the chickens are organized.
  • Scene cuts back to the chickens secret meeting (where ginger is now present)
  • Ginger leads the meeting; presents new plan for escape
  • Demonstrates their catapult idea
  • Mr. Tweedy is heard approaching, the chickens quickly break up their meeting… Mrs. Tweedy shouts for him… he returns back to the house
  • Ginger and the chickens get out of their nests
  • Ginger has a monologue about freedom.
  • DD:
  • Ginger: What about Edwina? How many more empty nests will it take?
  • Bunty: Perhaps it wouldn't be empty if she'd spent more time layin' and less time escapin'.
  • Ginger: So laying eggs all your life, and then getting plucked, stuffed and roasted is good enough for you, is it?
  • Babs: It's a livin'.
  • Ginger: You know what the problem is? The fences aren't just round the farm. They're up here in your heads. There's a better place out there somewhere beyond that hill, and it has wide open spaces and lots of trees, and grass. Can you imagine that? Cool, green grass.

Scene 3: Rocky's Arrival

By Raymond Sanchez

  • Ginger leaves the meeting, walks outside in distress
  • Rocky is introduced into the film as he haphazardly “flies” into the coop (making his grand entrance after crashing into the weather vane; he is knocked unconscious)
  • Ginger catches a poster of Rocky that floats from the sky; orders other hens to get this mysterious newcomer inside (Rocky); they do so hastily as Mr. Tweedy approaches the coop
  • The chickens realize who rocky is (comparing him to the poster); they find hope in the idea that he may be able to help them escape
  • Rocky gains consciousness, learns he has a sprained wing
  • Chaotic introductions/and banter are exchanged between the chickens and Rocky
  • Fowler is critical of Rocky
  • The chickens ask Rocky if he’s the rooster from the poster.
  • He egotistically confirms it’s him on the poster
  • Rocky’s song (EGO DRIVEN)
  • Chickens tell rocky they need him to teach them to fly, he flees the situation; ginger follows him outside
  • As they go back and forth a circus car pulls up
  • Ginger realizes Rocky is on the run
  • Ginger threatens to turn Rocky in because he tells Ginger he won't teach them to fly
  • Rocky ultimately agrees to teach the hens to fly in exchange for being kept hidden from those looking for him
  • He bunks with fowler - end of scene

Scene 4: So you wanna fly, huh?

By Naomi Mau

  • Training begins. “Fight for your Right” from Beastie Boys plays (with a few lyrics changes).
  • Mr. Tweedy notices the chickens exercising and calls this out to Mrs. Tweedy. He goes to take a better look towards the end of their bootcamp.
  • Ginger points out to Rocky that no one is actually flying, but everyone is actually exhausted from all the work.
  • Rocky begins “teaching how to fly” by having chickens run up a ramp, flap their wings, and fall into a pile of hay
  • Meanwhile, Nick and Fletcher make puns about the entire ordeal from downstage.
  • They hear an approaching noise: a large truck. Rocky thinks it's the circus, but it's actually the pie machine that has just arrived. They hide Rocky.
  • While Rocky is “hidden,” let's have a sort of pantomime scene where the chickens are frozen watching Mr. and Mrs. Tweedy.
  • Mac speaks to Ginger about the logistical issues of a chicken flying.
  • Fowler comments on Rocky’s character, and how he is “not to be trusted”
  • Scene ends dramatically

Scene 5: Mrs. Tweedy’s Homemade Chicken Pot Pies

By Naomi Mau

  • The next day. Ginger speaks with Rocky about the limited time they have to escape.
  • Rocky has already exchanged some deals with Nick and Fletcher to find the “thrust” to propel the chickens forward.
  • Chickens keep training.
  • They are suddenly given more feed. Chickens act excitedly. Mrs. Tweedy walks down stage, the runner curtain closes. Scene transitions into...
  • Mrs. Tweedy’s I Want Song. After the song, she walks off stage and the runner opens again to find that…
  • Ginger in the midst of enlightening the chickens as to why they are being fed extra. General uproar, but also general sadness.
  • Rocky cuts off Ginger’s speech and has a talk with her about motivation and encouragement.
  • This scene also ends dramatically with a sort of fight between Ginger and Rocky.

Scene 6: May I Have This Dance?

By Naomi Mau

  • Before the official, non-official dance begins, a small side bit where Nick and Fetcher bring the props (lights and a radio, decorations) for the dance and Rocky promises them an egg (a bit of a comic relief moment)
  • Rocky turns on the radio while Nick and Fetcher put up the lights and decorations. Chickens enter the scene, though some were already there (they bring on drinks/other props)
  • How about Funkytown from Lipps, inc (jukebox)
  • Ginger enters, surprised at everything. She walks around for a bit while chickens dance.
  • Ginger dances with Rocky
  • Song transitions to …some cheesy, slow dance number
  • We end with the Chicken Dance song (jukebox)
  • So… it would be three songs in total, and we would theoretically cut the pieces of the song that we want and mash/transition them together.

Scene 7: A Hot Situation

By Raymond Sanchez

  • Ginger and Rocky hear a noise outside (dance scene still going on);
  • The power goes out at the dance
  • Ginger leaves the coop to investigate, telling Rocky he better stay behind
  • Mrs. Tweedy and Mr. Tweedy are running Mrs. Tweedy’s new pie machine (reason the power went out)
  • Mrs. Tweedy asks Mr. Tweedy to get her a chicken so she can demonstrate how the pie machine works
  • Mr. Tweedy grabs Ginger who is being pursued by one of his dogs. Mr. Tweedy proclaims “I have a score to settle with you.”
  • Rocky learns Ginger has been taken; Fowler tells him to fly over there and save Ginger; Rocky (who is truly unable to fly) says they should try an element of surprise; he ziplines on a wire (with a hanger) into the room with the pie machine
  • Rocky and Ginger both end up in the pie machine; their feet become stuck in pie dough; they escape the dough but must avoid several more hazardous situations
  • Ultimately they end up in a pie ! Luckily still alive and limbs intact… they end up in the oven!.
  • Ginger lodges a wrench in the oven door to allow Rocky (who is seen getting hit with an array of pie ingredients) to escape the oven; the pie machine begins to malfunction
  • They run for their lives; ultimately escaping the situation
  • When a Mrs. Tweedy’s Pies sticker becomes stuck to Rocky; Ginger and Rocky learn of Mrs. Tweedy’s plan to turn all the chickens into pies
  • DD:
  • [they both continue running away from gears, with pieces flying, then they grab a chain, then swinging, then they fly in a box, then they both sigh, then they lay down, then putting the sticker on Rocky, saying, "Mrs. Tweedy's Chicken Pies", then walking to the sticker on Rocky, saying, "Mrs. Tweedy's Chicken Pies", then taking a sticker off]
  • Rocky: Ouch!
  • Ginger: We've got to show the others. Come on.

Scene 8: Escape Plan/Rocky’s Betrayal

By Isabella Selvitella

  • Rocky and Ginger run back to the pens to tell the rest of the chickens what’s going on
  • The other chickens are all still in the dark, and are all stressed and anxious
  • When Ginger and Rocky tell them what happened, mass panic ensues
  • Once Ginger calms them down, she tells them it’s ok, because Rocky is finally going to teach them how to fly
  • Rocky feels guilty, and goes back to the room he shares with Fowler
  • Once he gets there, Fowler tells him what he did was very brave and honorable
  • Fowler gives him the medal of bravery and salutes him
  • Fowler also gives Rocky the bunk and goes outside to sleep
  • Rocky is contemplating up on the roof, where he is joined by Ginger
  • They have a bit of an awkward interaction, but eventually they calm down and sit together
  • Ginger thanks him for all he’s done, and tells him that she’s never felt grass before
  • Rocky tells her that the outside world isn’t as exciting as she thinks it is
  • He wants to tell her the truth but he is too nervous
  • They touch hands, and both get nervous
  • Ginger leaves, and as she's walking away, Rocky calls her Ginger for the first time
  • The next morning all the hens are gathering around, and are excited to fly
  • Ginger hangs up the poster of Rocky
  • She then goes to his bunk to find him
  • Once she gets to the bunk, she finds the medal of valor, a nd the bottom half of Rocky’s poster
  • She realizes Rocky has left
  • She goes back out to there the other hens are waiting
  • She hangs up the bottom half of the poster
  • The new poster reveals that Rocky used a cannon in order to fly during the circus
  • Rocky has dug out under the fence, and has left
  • Back in the coop, the hens descend into chaos
  • They are all turning on each other and fighting

Scene 9: Chickens Can Fly/Pie in your Face

By Naomi Mau

  • Chickens decide to build a plane.
  • They enlist the help of Nick and Fetcher (and actually give them eggs)
  • Chickens begin constructing the flying machine while Mr. Tweedy simultaneously fixes the pie machine.
  • Overlapping desires of chickens and Mr. Tweedy
  • Jukebox or original song plays while: Mac gives directions, everyone gets to work
  • As Mr. Tweedy finishes the pie machine, the chickens panic
  • They are bought some time as Mr. Tweedy tells Mrs. Tweedy of his accomplishment.
  • Mr. Tweedy goes to get ALL the chickens
  • More panic
  • The plane is finished, but Fowler cannot fly it!
  • Small debate about whether Fowler can fly the plane or not.
  • Fowler flies over a ramp (but fails as the ramp needs to be higher).
  • Ginger goes to prop the ramp.
  • Mr. Tweedy interferes with Ginger
  • Rocky returns to help Ginger
  • The ramp works: Ginger and Rocky quickly grab hole of the rope attached to the plane.
  • Mr. and Mrs. Tweedy also grab a hold of the rope until they fall back onto the farm.
  • As the plane flies away, Mrs. Tweedy falls into the pie machine ultimately destroying the pie machine.
  • Mr. Tweedy stands there to watch.
  • End of scene.
