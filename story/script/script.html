Scene 1: Freedom, Where Art Thou?

(Written by Isabella Selvitella. Edited by Ester Sam)

EXT.

All is quiet, then, the Mission Impossible music begins to play. The lights come on, and we see a series of shots of the chickens, led by Ginger, planning and executing elaborate escapes that never quite work. Mr. Tweedy and his guard dogs chase the chickens. Eventually, Mr. Tweedy catches Ginger and throws her into solitary confinement.

MR. TWEEDY

"That is the last time you make a fool out of me! Now, you will sit in there until you stop this escaping nonsense!” Turns to the rest of the chickens, “Now let this be a warning to the rest of you! NO CHICKEN ESCAPES FROM TWEEDY FARMS!”

EXT. CHICKEN COOP - DAY

GINGER is released from solitary, joins the rest of the chickens for egg count.

BABS

Morning, Ginger. Back from holiday?

GINGER

(IRRITATED)

I wasn’t on holiday, Babs. I was in solitary confinement.

BABS

Oh, it’s nice to get a bit of time to yourself, isn’t it?

MRS. TWEEDY arrives, scaring all the chickens

MRS. TWEEDY

Now, would you look at that. No eggs. Off to the chopping block with you!

MRS. TWEEDY discovers that EDWINA has not produced any eggs, so takes her off to be killed.

GINGER watches helplessly from the rooftop. She sees birds flying overhead, and wishes that they could all escape.

GINGER

We have got to get out of here.

QUEUE Ginger's I Want Song: Written by Molly Hafer: Placement chosen by Producer Raymond Sanchez

MAC approaches.

MAC

Ginger! Are we still on?

GINGER

(determined)

Oh, we are still on.

(Looks at MAC)

Spread the word, Mac. Meeting tonight in Hut 17.

Front curtain closes.

End Scene.

Scene 2​: Secret Meeting

(Written by Isabella Selvitella. Edited by Naomi Mau)

INT. HUT 17

The scene opens downstage center (in front of closed front curtain) with all of the hens gathering in Hut 17. They wait to hear of GINGER’s latest plan. Before the meeting, she speaks with NICK and FLETCHER, the rats, off to the side.

NICK

You called? Nick and..

FETCHER

Fetcher!

NICK

At your service. Now, what can we do for you, miss?

GINGER

We need some more things.

NICK

Of course, miss. But before we discuss what you need, let’s discuss payment.

GINGER

(Slightly annoyed)

What is it you want?

NICK AND FETCHER

(together)

Eggs.

GINGER

Absolutely not. Those are too valuable!

NICK

Well, then, pleasure doing business with you madame, good night!

NICK and FETCHER walk exit left. GINGER stands there a moment, looking defeated. She returns to the meeting. The chickens are down low to the ground. They ad. lib. a meeting, then hit an interesting tableau. Front curtain opens.

On a raised platform is MR. TWEEDY’s office where MRS. TWEEDY is viewing an ad for a pie machine. Meanwhile, MR. TWEEDY is staring out a window, spying on the chickens.

MR. TWEEDY

I don’t trust those chickens. They are up to something.

MRS. TWEEDY

(Absorbed in the ad and paying no heed to her husband)

Would you shut up! They’re chickens, they are too dumb to organize.

MRS. TWEEDY freezes in tableau as MR. TWEEDY’s ​They’re Organizing song begins.​ At the end of the song, the front curtain closes.

The chicken meeting breaks tableau and comes into full action. GINGER is explaining the latest escape plan.

GINGER

It’s a catapult, ladies! And, no, Helia, we are not going to start a fire! Look, Mac is in charge ofmechanics and we will help assemble the catapult!

BUNTY

(Looking out stage left, where MR. TWEEDY enters shortly.)

Mr. Tweedy is coming!

All the chickens immediately start tidying up and rushing into their beds.

MR. TWEEDY opens the door to the hut and sees all the hens asleep in their bed. He looks confused, but closes the door and walks back to the main house.

The chickens wait until they hear MR. TWEEDY walk away before getting back out of their beds.

GINGER stands up, and looks out at all the hens.

GINGER

We can’t keep living here! In constant fear. The only permissions we get are to sleep, eat, and lay eggs. And our precious eggs are stolen from us every day! Don’t you all want to be free? To feel the sun on your face and the grass under your feet?

BUNTY

But why do we need those things? We are taken care of here. If we leave, all that goes away.

GINGER

What about Edwina? They took her away from us. How many open nests will it take?

BUNTY

Maybe it wouldn’t be empty if she’d spent more time layin’ and less time escapin’!

The chickens gasp in shock! BUNTY shrugs.

GINGER

So laying eggs all your life, and then getting plucked, stuffed, and roasted is good enough for you, is it?

BABS

It’s a livin’.

GINGER

You know what the problem is? The fences aren’t just round the farm. They’re up here in your heads. There’s a better place out there somewhere beyond that hill, and it has wide open spaces and lots of trees and grass. Can you imagine that? Cool, green grass.

None of the chickens look convinced and they turn away from her, ready to sleep. Out of frustration, GINGER exits left. Lights out.

End Scene.

Scene 3​: A Rocky Arrival

(Written by Vipul Gupta. Edited by Naomi Mau)

EXT. HUT 17 - NIGHT

The chickens exit the scene, the front curtain is opened to reveal Ginger outside of all the coops, which are lined in a neat row behind her. She walks downstage, center. Frustrated, she punches the fence.

FOWLER hears her punch the fence and looks out of a coop. He walks out from the coop.

GINGER

Heaven help us.

A scream is heard in the distance. GINGER and FOWLER react and look up to see ROCKY the rooster “flying” above the chicken farm.

ROCKY

FREEDOM!

ROCKY crashes into the weather vane and falls inside the fence in front of GINGER. He is knocked unconscious.

FOWLER

(In disgust)

No decency, this one.

GINGER catches a torn flyer that shows ROCKY flying with the words “Rocky the Flying Rooster”. She cheats out towards the house to demonstrate the poster to the audience. GINGER suddenly gets an idea.

GINGER

That’s it! Get him inside, quickly!

GINGER and FOWLER rush ROCKY inside HUT 17 (the center chicken coop).

MR. TWEEDY walks outside patrolling the farm with DOG 1 and DOG 2. They walk around the coops with a flashlight before exiting stage right.

After he exits, the chickens rush to Hut 17 one by one. GINGER and the CHICKENS stand around ROCKY’s unconscious body.

GINGER

This is our ticket out of here everyone.

BUNTY

Don’t look like much.

BABS

We’ll make posters.

GINGER

No Babs, what’s on the poster? We’ll fly our way out of here!

BABS

He must be very important to have his picture taken.

ROCKY starts to come to his senses. Waking up, he is confused and dazed about his situation.

ROCKY

(Dazed)

Where am I? Who are you? Ouch! (he reaches for his wing) What’s wrong with my wing.

GINGER

You took a rather nasty fall and sprained your wing. Mac over here will wrap it up for you.

BABS

I made a bandage!

(She hands MAC a quickly knitted bandage.)

MAC wraps the bandage around ROCKY’s wing as the chickens chatter about the fascinating flying rooster. They all speak at the same time (ad. lib.)

ROCKY

Whoa. Whoa. Let's back up and start from the top. Where am I?

GINGER

You're right. Excuse us, we are just very excited you’re here. This is a chicken farm.

BABS

And we're the chickens.

FOWLER

I’m not too sure about this one. He has a funny look about him.

ROCKY

Hey, what's up with Grandpa?

GINGER

Oh, don't mind him, Mister... ?

ROCKY

Rocky. Rocky the Rhode Island Red. Rhodes for short.

GINGER

Is this you Mr. Rhodes?

(She holds up the flyer)

ROCKY

(Nervously)

Uh, why do you ask..in particular?

GINGER

You see, us chickens are in a rather desperate situation. If it is you, you might be able to help us!

All the chickens stare intently at ROCKY.

ROCKY

Well I must be what you’ve been waiting for all along, because that’s me.

Rocky’s song (EGO DRIVEN)

The chickens all clap for ROCKY and chat excitedly. He speaks, simultaneously making his way towards the door.

ROCKY

(Confidently)

See, I can’t stay in one place for too long. I lived in a barnyard for a while, but couldn’t do it permanently. The open road is more my style. In fact, they call me the Lone Ranger where I come from.

GINGER

I knew it could happen. We are going to fly over that fence. And you’re going to show us how!

ROCKY

Uhhmm... hold on. You want me to teach you to fly?

GINGER

Yes. Don’t you see it, we can ​all​ escape out of here if we can fly.

ROCKY

Oh I couldn’t do that. In fact, I should be on my way, actually (pauses and cranes his neck to listen) that’s the open road calling my name.

ROCKY runs out the door quickly. He quickly walks downstage, slowly pacing from left to right. GINGER follows. The lights dim for the chickens upstage, who hit a tableau.

GINGER

Mr. Rhodes! Wait, please! Let me explain our situation more clearly. We lay eggs every day. And when we can’t anymore, we’re killed. Do you understand? We’re dead chickens if we stay here!

ROCKY

(Distracted by a non-existent noise in the distance)

It's a tough world doll face.

GINGER

Need I repeat that they kill us when we can’t lay eggs anymore! Escaping is our only option.

ROCKY stops in his tracks and turns to GINGER.

ROCKY

Everyone? The entire--

GINGER

Yes! That’s what I’ve been saying this entire time!

ROCKY

You’re crazy! Pulling off a stunt like that would be suicide.

GINGER

Where there is a will, there is a way.

ROCKY

Yes, and I’ll be leaving that way.

ROCKY walks stage left to exit. There are two bright “headlights” shine before him, he runs back center. The sound of a bus horn is heard. ROCKY panics and runs to GINGER.

ROCKY

Please! Hide me!

GINGER grabs his wing and takes him to the right side of the farthest right coop upstage. A bus enters downstage left. The bus reads “Circus.” CIRCUS MAN steps out of the bus and peers out into the house. MR. TWEEDY enters downstage right and stairs at CIRCUS MAN. The downstage lights dim and the lights near GINGER and ROCKY increase in brightness.

GINGER

So, you’re from the circus and you’re on the run aren’t you? There’s no reason I shouldn’t turn you in right now.

ROCKY

You’re crazy, do you know what they’ll do to me if they catch me?

GINGER

Like you said, it's a tough world. We all have our own problems.

ROCKY

I just realized, I don’t think I like you.

GINGER

I don’t care. You’re going to show us how to fly or I’m going to start screaming right now.

ROCKY

No!

CUT TO:

The man from the bus, MR. TWEEDY, and MRS. TWEEDY are all stage center. They are all gathered at the front door of the house, and the man has already asked if they had seen ROCKY. The chickens move back into their own coops upstage.

MRS. TWEEDY

He’s valuable? Get the torch Mr. Tweedy.

CUT TO:

ROCKY and GINGER stage center. They are still in the same place.

ROCKY

Listen Doll Face. I’m not going back to that life. I’m a Lone Free Ranger.

GINGER

Exactly! That’s what we all want for ourselves. Freedom.

They can then see light from MRS. TWEEDY’s flashlight as she turns it on. She and MR. TWEEDY enter the gate at center.

GINGER

How about this. If you teach us to fly, we’ll keep you hidden here at the farm. Deal?

ROCKY

Deal.

GINGER then rushes ROCKY to the chicken coup stage left, and knocks on the door. They are dragged inside quickly. MRS. TWEEDY hears sound coming from the area of the coup. She opens the roof to see all the chickens sleeping and closes it. GINGER opens a small box in which ROCKY was hiding in, and takes him out.

GINGER

Now that we’ve held up our part of the agreement. Tomorrow, first thing in the morning, we’ll be ready to learn how to fly.

ROCKY

Sure thing! I’ll teach you everything I know. Now, where should I sleep?

All the chickens shout “Me! Me!” from their bunks.

CUT TO:

FOWLER and ROCKY sleeping in the same bunk, center stage.

FOWLER

Outrageous! Absolutely outrageous! Sharing a bunk with me, a senior officer?

ROCKY

Hey, I don’t like this just as much as you pal. Is that smell your breath?

FOWLER

Ridiculous!

Panning shot away from the chicken coup from the outside.

End Scene.

Scene 4​: So You Want to Fly, Huh?

(Written by Vipul Gupta. Edited by Naomi Mau)

EXT. CHICKEN COOP-MORNING

The chicken moves are moved downstage while a platform is brought out upstage. MR. TWEEDY’s house is on the platform. Pan onto outside of HUT 12, where ROCKY is standing in front of the hut and all the other chickens are gathered around listening to him.

ROCKY

Flying requires three very important qualities. Hard work, perseverance, and ... hard work.

FOWLER

You said hard work twice!

ROCKY

Well, it takes twice the amount of hard work as perseverance to fly.

FOWLER

Nonsense!

FOWLER enters his hut mad and closes the door, hard.

ROCKY

Okay. Let’s get started everyone!

With “Fight For Your Right” playing in the background, the chickens start exercising together as a group. This includes moving their wings back and forth, raising their wings up, and doing pushups. MR. TWEEDY sees them doing push-ups from his house upstage. He rushes to show MRS. TWEEDY.

MR. TWEEDY

Mrs. Tweedy! Look, I told you. The chickens are organized. They’re, ... oh my.

The chickens, hearing a small commotion in MR. TWEEDY’s house, return to innocently pecking the ground and doing chickenly things. MRS. TWEEDY hits MR. TWEEDY in the head with her clipboard and walks away. MR. TWEEDY enters a tableau upstage, reading the newspaper.

CUT TO:

Back to chickens doing exercise with ROCKY giving instructions.

ROCKY

And left, one, two, three. Right, one, two, three. Down, make little circles.

All the chickens start turning in circles. GINGER notices ROCKY, who is stage left, taking a bath and getting a massage from other chickens. GINGER crosses over to ROCKY.

GINGER

I thought you were teaching us how to fly. It seems like we’re just going around in circles.

GINGER points to the chickens turning around in circles and getting dizzy.

ROCKY

That’s what I’m doing. (ROCKY to everyone) Hey, cut it out everyone, I’m getting dizzy watching you guys. (To GINGER) I think they’re ready to fly now. (To all the chickens) Alright everyone, warm up your wings, let’s fly.

One of the chickens, stage right, starts running towards a wooden ramp, and starts flapping its wings and jumps off the ramp.

CUT TO:

NICK and FLETCHER crawl out downstage left to watch the chickens.

NICK

Well Fletcher, let’s go talk to Attilla the hen shall we?

The chicken lands right next to NICK and FLETCHER, shocking the rats. More chickens land near NICK and FLETCHER, and the rats run around to avoid being squashed.

FLETCHER

It’s raining hen!

BABS comes screaming and falling down next to FLETCHER and NICK. She lands and continues her knitting.

NICK

What are you all up to?

BABS

We’re learning to fly!

NICK

Fletch, they’re going to hurt themselves!

FLETCHER

(pause) Wanna watch?

Both NICK and FLETCHER rush to create a little set up of a couple chairs right near the chickens and sit to watch the chickens attempt to fly. Downstage right, MAC and a few other chickens attempt to get a head start off a treadmill, however MAC goes face first into the ground when boosted off the treadmill.

NICK

Watch your eggs! (laughing)

CUT TO:

Another chicken attempting to fly, center stage. It is wrapped in rope, and the other chicken quickly spins the chicken out of the rope to give it a boost in the air. The chicken ends up spinning and falling to the ground.

FLETCHER

Definitely scrambled eggs! (laughing)

CUT TO:

ROCKY on the top of a roof, center stage with many of the chickens rushing off it one by one attempting to fly.

ROCKY

Go! Go! Go!

NICK

(in the background) Poultry in motion! (laughing)

Pan to GINGER on top of the roof, runs and jumps off the roof flapping her wings. She thinks she is flying for a few seconds, then realizes she has just landed on a large pile of her fellow chickens who also tried flying off the roof. The entire pile falls down. NICK and FLETCHER laugh in the background. All the chickens grumble away to their huts, dejected and exhausted.

ROCKY

Good work ladies! The pain you all are feeling right now is progress. It’s a good thing! Just keep the faith. Ducky, I think you flew four feet! Great job!

NICK

Four feet from the roof to the dirt! (laughing with FLETCHER)

ROCKY

(mumbling to himself)

cheese-eating, little ...

Suddenly, the ground starts shaking (indicated by the chickens wobbling around and falling down). An earthquake sound is heard along with the sound of a truck.

ROCKY

Whoa! What’s that? The ground is shaking. That can’t be good. Are we alright, is everything alright? (to GINGER)

A massive truck enters the farm upstage left, and it’s bright headlights shine on the chickens looking at the truck.

ROCKY

Ahh! It must be the circus. Quick! Hide me!

GINGER

Let’s go!

ROCKY and GINGER rush away to FOWLER’s hut.

CUT TO:

FOWLER polishing a medal in his hut. GINGER quickly enters the hut.

FOWLER

Excuse me, this is an officer’s quarters! Out of here immediately sir!

ROCKY

Give it a rest, old man.

GINGER shoves ROCKY into a metal contraption and closes the door so he cannot be found. GINGER leaves the hut.

CUT TO:

MR. TWEEDY exits tableau. MRS. TWEEDY enters upstage right (from atop the platform). The truck is up center and all the chickens are staring at it. The words “POULTRY PRODUCTS” can be seen on the side of the truck. The truck then opens and automatically moves out a massive metal machine in crates carried by chains. MR. TWEEDY directs the truck to put the machine in the house and the chickens are standing there stunned, not knowing what the contraption is. The truck closes and starts to drive off, as the chickens look on with binoculars at the machine.

MR. TWEEDY notices the chickens looking with binoculars.

MR. TWEEDY

It’s all in your head, it’s all in your head.

CUT TO:

MRS. TWEEDY Standing outside the household, closes the door, rubs her hands and smiles at the chickens. She walks off stage right.

CUT TO:

GINGER and MAC standing at the fence.

GINGER

This can’t be good Mac. Whatever is in those crates is probably for us.

MAC

Yes Ginger. I hate to bring more bad news, but I don’t know if us chickens are built for flying. According to my calculations, there’s something we’re missing.

GINGER

I saw him though. He flew directly over me, over the entire fence.

MAC

I believe you GINGER. We may need to see it for ourselves. It could answer my questions.

GINGER

Yes, you’re right. We’ve been doing our best all week, maybe his wing is better. I’ll go talk to him.

GINGER walks over to FOWLER’s hut. The metal door is open and the space where she hid ROCKY is empty. FOWLER is stage right.

GINGER

(to FOWLER)

Huh! Where is he?

FOWLER

They didn’t give me this medal for nothing.

GINGER

(rolls eyes) “I don’t know” would have been sufficient.

FOWLER

(as GINGER is about to leave) Careful GINGER. You cannot trust that fool.

GINGER

That “fool” is our only way out of this madhouse.

GINGER leaves the hut. Scene is ended in a very dramatic, suspenseful fashion.

End Scene.

Scene 5​: Mrs. Tweedy’s Homemade Pies

(Written by Paolo Octoman. Edited by Ester Sam and Naomi Mau)

INT. CHICKEN COOP-NIGHT

Two coops are set upstage, left and right. MR. TWEEDY’s house, the platform, and the trucky are striked. ROCKY is stage center surrounded by the chickens.

ROCKY

And the pig says to the horse, "Hey, fella, why the long face?"

The chickens laugh in response to the punchline. Soft harmonica jazz music begins playing in the background. ROCKY takes a drink of water from a cocktail-shaped funnel.

ROCKY

Oh oh look, look.

ROCKY plucks one of his own feathers and places it in his drink

ROCKY

Cocktail!

Everyone laughs in response. ROCKY takes another drink. GINGER enters stage left.

ROCKY

(Nervous)

So, um, anyway... Remember those flying tips tomorrow. They're very important. Keep thinking those flighty thoughts.

The crowd of chickens, minus GINGER disperse and the music lightly continues.

ROCKY

They're swell chicks. They really are. Look at what Babs made me. A beak warmer.

ROCKY pulls out a knitted beak garment and puts it on.

ROCKY

(Muffled)

Isn't that the cutest?

(Removes the muffler)

And that Bunty. She really packs a pu-. Is there a problem here?

GINGER

(Stern)

Have we flown over that fence?

ROCKY

Not quite

GINGER

Then there's a problem.

ROCKY

Hey. Good things come to those who wait, doll face.

GINGER

(Irritated)

My name is Ginger!

CUT TO:

EXT.

ROCKY splashes his face with water and adjusts his hair from a “sink” outside.

GINGER

Okay, how long did it take you?

ROCKY

To do what?

GINGER

To learn how to fly.

ROCKY

Apples and oranges, baby doll. I'm gifted. They're not. You can't compare the two, okay? The point is, these things take time.

GINGER

Which we are rapidly running out of. We haven't even lifted off the ground. Why?

MAC

(O.S.)

Thrust!

GINGER and ROCKY

What?

ZOOM: MAC

MAC enters stage left, giving lines as she enters.

MAC

I went over my calculations, and I figured the key element we're missing is thrust.

ROCKY

I-I didn't get a word of that.

MAC

Thrust! Other birds like ducks and geese, when they take off, what do they have? Thrust.

ROCKY

I swear she ain't usin' real words.

GINGER

She said we need more thrust.

ROCKY

Oh, thrust. Well, of course, we need thrust. Thrust and flying are like this.

ROCKY

(HOLDS UP TWO FINGERS)

That's flying and that's thrust.

GINGER

(to MAC)

Would you excuse us?

MAC

Aye.

GINGER pulls ROCKY by his injured arm off to the side.

ROCKY

Ah, the wing, the wing, the wing!

GINGER

(Threatening)

If we don't see some results by tomorrow, the deal is off, and you're on your own. No more hiding. The farmers will find you, and it's back to the circus, flyboy!

ROCKY

You're only the first chick I ever met with the shell still on. Sleep tight, angel face. The Rock's on the case.

ROCKY clicks his tongue, finger-guns, and winks at GINGER before walking away, exiting stage right.

GINGER

Ginger!

GINGER throws a scarf at ROCKY’S face as he walks away. The lights dim.

FADE TO:

EXT. CHICKEN COOP ROOFTOP - MORNING

FOWLER

(O.S.)

Cock-a-doodle-doo!

CUT TO:

INT. CHICKEN COOP - Morning

The lights fade in. Chickens exit their coops.

ROCKY, NICK, and FLETCHER are conversing downstage left, slightly away from the chicken coops.

NICK

We slipped into the farmer's room, all quiet like.

FLETCHER

Like a fish.

NICK

Yeah, and we... "Like a fish"? You stupid Norbert. Anyway, here it is. El merchandiso.

NICK hands ROCKY a bag.

FLETCHER

That’s Spanish

GINGER approaches the three.

GINGER

What are these two crooks doing here?

ROCKY

So, you know each other.

FLETCHER

She don't think we're valuable.

ROCKY

Guys, you are without a doubt the sneakiest, most light-fingered thieving parasites I've ever met.

NICK

Oh, don't, don't. Stop it. So, uh, how about those eggs?

GINGER

Eggs? Don't tell me you promised them-

ROCKY

(Winks)

Yep! Promised them every egg I lay this month.

NICK

And when can we expect the first installment?

ROCKY

I'm brewing one up as we speak, guys. I'll keep you posted.

NICK

Pleasure doing business with you, sir.

NICK and FLETCHER turn around and walk away, exiting left.

NICK

(Whispers FLETCHER)

Sucker.

GINGER

You lied to them.

ROCKY

I didn't lie, doll face. I just omitted certain truths. I'll give them exactly what I promised.

GINGER

Which is nothing.

ROCKY

Which is what I’ll give them

GINGER

And what will you give us?

ROCKY pulls a belt out of the bag and stretches it in front of GINGER

ROCKY

Thrust!

CUT TO:

A nervous BUNTY is on top of a cart, center stage, being loaded on a slingshot aimed toward the fence (stage right). NICK and FLETCHER and the chickens are stage right.

PLAY: Dramatic music

ROCKY

You okay, sweetheart? Good, good. Now this is just a little more helper. Something to get you going. It's a thrust exercise.

FLETCHER

The tension is killing me.

NICK

It’s gonna kill her!

ROCKY

(LAUGHING)

Release!

The chickens release BUNTY. BUNTY’S cart shoots toward the fence.

GINGER

(to herself)

C’mon, flap!

BUNTY starts frantically flapping her arms. A rope connected to the cart reaches its length, stopping the cart, and sending BUNTY in the air. She hits the fence and it launches her back in the air.

NICK

(LAUGHING)

Is that your first offense?

POV: NICK AND FLETCHER

NICK, FLETCHER, and BUNTY all scream before she lands on them.

CUT TO:

Several chickens move out of the way as BUNTY, smushed with NICK and FLETCHER, roll past crashing.

ROCKY

(AWKWARDLY)

Oops.

The bell rings. All the chickens scramble to their roll call.

BABS

Roll call! I haven't laid any eggs. Three days and not a single one. Oh, no!

GINGER

Why didn't you tell us, Babs?!

BABS

We've been so busy with the flying.

ROCKY

Hide me!

GINGER

(IRRITATED)

Hide yourself!

The chickens order themselves in roll call.

Sinister music begins playing as MRS. TWEEDY enters stage right, opens the fence and enters the coop.

The shot shifts back and forth between MRS. TWEEDY’S steps and the visibly-anxious BABS as MRS. TWEEDY gets closer.

POV: BABS

BABS looks up to see MRS. TWEEDY with an evil smile.

NICK and FLETCHER look around. Upstage, GINGER and ROCKY enter tossing each other the football. The other chickens join in.

NICK turns on the radio beside him.

NICK

Fancy some scrambled eggs Fletcher? Let’s go play some game!

Hearing the music, the football game quickly turns into a dance. The dance transitions into the final bows of the actors.

First round of bows:​ BABS, BUNTY, and EDWINA, followed by extra chickens

Second round of bows:​ FOWLER, MAC and CIRCUS MAN

Third round of bows:​ NICK and FLETCHER

Fourth round of bows:​ MR. TWEEDY, MRS. TWEEDY, and DOGS

Last round of bows:​ ROCKY, then GINGER, then all actors.

The music fades and the actors take one last bow.

End Scene.
